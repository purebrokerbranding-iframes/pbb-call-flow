1 Pattern Interrupt Opener

▼
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with Pure Broker Branding — I know this is completely out of the blue, and I'm sure you're busy, so I'll be super quick. Fair enough?"
💡 Pro Tip: Tonality is everything. Drop your voice slightly at "fair enough?" — it signals confidence, not desperation. Pause after they say "sure" to create tension.

2 Reason for the Call

▼
"The reason I'm calling is — we help [industry] professionals build a brand that actually gets attention and brings in clients, without relying on word of mouth or expensive ad agencies. I looked at your current online presence and thought we might be able to help."
💡 Pro Tip: Say "I looked at your current online presence" — it proves you did homework, not a spray-and-pray dialer.

3 Discovery — Pain & Situation

▼

Ask 2–3 questions. Match to their industry:

  • "How are you currently generating new leads or clients?"
  • "When someone searches you online, are you happy with what they find?"
  • "Do you have a website and brand that you feel represents your business well?"
  • "Are your referral partners actually sending you consistent business, or is it hit or miss?"
  • "How are you standing out from every other agent in your market right now?"
  • "When a prospect compares you to a competitor online, what do they see?"
  • "How are you building trust with prospects before the first meeting?"
  • "Do your high-net-worth prospects research you online before committing?"
  • "What does your digital first impression say about your advisory practice?"
  • "In a market with so many agents, how are you differentiating yourself?"
  • "When a seller checks you out before the listing appointment, what do they find?"
  • "Are you getting leads from your online presence, or mostly referrals and cold outreach?"
💡 Pro Tip: Listen more than you talk here. The prospect should be doing 70% of the talking. Every answer is ammo for the gap statement.

4 Gap Statement — Create Urgency

▼
"So it sounds like you're doing [X], but you're not seeing the kind of leads/growth/trust you want. That gap between where you are and where you want to be — that's exactly what we fix."

Industry-specific gap amplifiers:

"Right now, your competitors who invest in branding are the ones getting the referrals, the Google searches, and the trust — before the prospect even picks up the phone. That's the gap."
"High-net-worth clients aren't Googling 'financial advisor near me' and picking the first one. They're vetting you. If your online presence doesn't match your expertise, you're losing six-figure clients before you even know they were looking."
"Every seller has 10 agents in their inbox. The one who looks the most professional online — polished brand, strong website, real authority — that's who gets the listing appointment. If that's not you, it's your competitor."
💡 Pro Tip: Pause after the gap statement. Silence creates urgency. Let them feel the gap.

5 Soft Pitch — The Bridge

▼
"What we do is build premium brands, websites, and marketing systems that position you as the top choice in your market — so when prospects are comparing options, you're the obvious pick. We've done this for [industry] professionals across the country."
💡 Pro Tip: Don't pitch features. Pitch the outcome: "you become the obvious choice." Features come in the strategy call.

6 Call-to-Action — Book the Strategy Call

▼
"I'd love to show you what this could look like for your business. It's a quick 15-minute strategy call — no pressure, no sales pitch — I'll walk you through exactly what we'd do for you. Does [day] or [day] work better this week?"
💡 Pro Tip: Always give two options (binary close). "Does Tuesday or Thursday work?" is 3x more effective than "When are you free?"

7 Confirm & Lock In

▼
"Perfect — I've got you down for [day/time]. I'll send over a quick confirmation with a link. Looking forward to it, [Name]."
💡 Pro Tip: Immediately send a calendar invite + confirmation. Speed-to-confirm reduces no-shows by 40%.

"I'm not interested." Universal

▼
"Totally fair — most people say that before they see what we actually do. Can I ask one quick question? If there was a way to consistently attract better clients without relying on referrals alone, would that be worth a 15-minute conversation?"

"I don't have the budget." Universal

▼
"I hear you — and honestly, budget is the #1 thing our clients worried about before they started. But here's what they found: not investing in their brand was costing them more in lost deals than the investment itself. The strategy call is free — let me at least show you the ROI potential."

"I already have a website." Universal

▼
"That's great — having a website is step one. But the question is: is it actually working for you? Is it bringing in leads, building trust, and making people choose you over competitors? That's the difference between a website and a brand that converts. Let me show you what that looks like."

"Send me some info / an email." Universal

▼
"Absolutely — I can do that. But just so I send you the right stuff, can I ask one quick thing? What would need to be true for you to say 'this is worth exploring'? That way I don't waste your time with generic info."
💡 Pro Tip: This reframes "send info" from a brush-off into a qualifying conversation. If they give a real answer, you're back in the game.

"I need to think about it." Universal

▼
"Totally respect that — I'd never want you to rush into anything. Just so I understand, when you say 'think about it,' is it the investment, the timing, or are you not sure this is the right fit? That way I can get you the right info to think it over."

"I'm too busy right now." Universal

▼
"I completely understand — you're busy because you're good at what you do. That's actually exactly why this works: we build everything for you. No homework, no DIY. The strategy call is 15 minutes, and we handle 100% of the execution. What does your Thursday look like?"

"I've been burned before by agencies." Universal

▼
"I'm sorry to hear that — and honestly, that's one of the biggest reasons people come to us. Most agencies are generic, slow, and don't understand your industry. We specialize exclusively in [insurance/financial/real estate] professionals. That's all we do. The strategy call will feel different — I promise. And if it doesn't, you can tell me and we'll part ways. Fair?"

"I get all my business from referrals." Universal

▼
"That's awesome — referrals mean you're great at what you do. But here's the thing: even your referrals are Googling you before they call. If your online presence doesn't match the reputation your referral partner gave you, you're losing deals you don't even know about. We make sure when people look you up, they're blown away."

"How are you different from other agencies?" Universal

▼
"Great question. Three things: 1) We only work with [industry] professionals — we're not a generalist agency trying to figure out your market. 2) We build everything — brand, website, content, marketing — as one system, not piecemeal. 3) Our clients actually see results because we focus on conversion, not just making things look pretty."

"I can just do it myself / use DIY tools." Universal

▼
"You definitely could — Canva, Wix, all that stuff exists. But let me ask: would you tell your clients to handle their own [insurance/finances/real estate] because YouTube tutorials exist? Probably not. Your expertise is in your business — ours is in making your brand the most trusted name in your market. Let us do what we're best at so you can do what you're best at."

"My carrier provides marketing." Insurance

▼
"They do — and so does every other agent's carrier. That's the problem. When every agent in your market has the same carrier templates, who stands out? Nobody. A custom brand is what separates the top 1% of agents from the rest. That's what we build."

"My book of business is big enough." Insurance

▼
"That's great — but are you retaining those clients as well as you'd like? A strong brand doesn't just attract new clients, it keeps existing ones loyal. When your clients see competitors marketing to them, your brand is the reason they stay."

"I only work with referrals from agents." Insurance

▼
"That's a great channel — but even the agents referring you are looking you up online before they send their clients your way. If your brand doesn't look the part, you're missing referrals you'll never know about. We make sure your digital presence closes before you even pick up the phone."

"Compliance won't approve it." Financial

▼
"Totally understand — compliance is real. We've worked with advisors across multiple BDs and RIAs. We build everything to be compliance-friendly and work directly with your compliance team if needed. You won't have to worry about that part."

"My BD provides a website." Financial

▼
"They do — and it looks exactly like every other advisor's at your BD. When a prospect with $2M to invest is comparing three advisors and all three have the same cookie-cutter BD site, who do they pick? The one with a real brand. That's the edge we give you."

"My AUM is big enough, I don't need marketing." Financial

▼
"That's impressive — and I'd imagine you didn't build that by accident. But here's the question: if you could attract higher-net-worth clients or build a recognizable brand that commands premium fees, wouldn't that be worth exploring? We're not about volume — we're about positioning you for the next level."

"I just use Zillow / Realtor.com for leads." Real Estate

▼
"Those platforms work — but you're renting leads from a platform where you're competing with 20 other agents for the same prospect. A strong brand means people come to YOU directly. Zillow leads cost money and loyalty. Your brand builds equity."

"My brokerage handles my marketing." Real Estate

▼
"Your brokerage markets the brokerage — not you. When you leave or switch, that marketing goes away. Your personal brand follows you everywhere. The top producers in every market have their own brand identity. That's what we build."

"The market is slow right now." Real Estate

▼
"That's exactly when you should be investing in your brand. When the market is slow, the agents who show up looking professional and trustworthy get the few deals that are happening. When the market comes back, you'll be the first name people think of. The agents who build their brand in a downturn dominate the upswing."

Sales Playbook — 10 Principles

Principle 01

Tonality Over Script

How you say it matters 10x more than what you say. A confident, conversational tone creates trust. Reading a script creates distance. Master the message, then forget the words.

Principle 02

Ask, Don't Tell

Questions control conversations. The person asking questions is always in control. Discovery isn't a checklist — it's a conversation that makes the prospect sell themselves on needing help.

Principle 03

Silence Is a Weapon

After you make a key point or ask a closing question — shut up. The first person who talks loses. Silence creates pressure that makes the prospect process and respond honestly.

Principle 04

Sell the Meeting, Not the Product

On a cold call, you're not selling branding services. You're selling 15 minutes. Lower the barrier. The strategy call sells itself — your job is just to get them there.

Principle 05

Pattern Interrupt First

The first 7 seconds decide if they listen or hang up. Break their pattern with honesty: "I know this is out of the blue." It disarms them because it's the opposite of what every other caller does.

Principle 06

Pain Over Features

Nobody buys branding. They buy the solution to their pain: lost deals, weak online presence, blending in with competitors. Find the pain, amplify it, then present the bridge.

Principle 07

The Binary Close

"Does Tuesday or Thursday work?" is 3x more effective than "When are you free?" Always give two options. It assumes the close and eliminates the "let me check and get back to you" escape.

Principle 08

Objections = Interest

A prospect who objects is a prospect who's engaged. Someone truly not interested just hangs up. Every objection is an invitation to go deeper. Welcome them.

Principle 09

Speed to Follow-Up

Send the calendar invite within 60 seconds of booking. Text a confirmation within 5 minutes. Speed signals professionalism and eliminates buyer's remorse. Every minute you wait, the close rate drops.

Principle 10

Detach From the Outcome

Desperation kills deals. If you need every call to convert, you'll push too hard. Approach every call like you don't need it. Paradoxically, that confidence is exactly what makes people say yes.
