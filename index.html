1 Start Here — Open the Call

▼
"Hey [Name], appreciate you hopping on — I've been looking forward to this. I want this call to be 100% about your business and what you're trying to build, so I'm gonna ask you a few questions up front and then show you exactly what we'd put together for you. Cool?"
💡 Pro Tip: Set the frame early — you're leading this call. "Cool?" gets a micro-yes and gives you control of the conversation from the start.

2 Set the Agenda

▼
"Here's how this'll go — I'll learn about where your business is right now, where you want it to be, and what's getting in the way. Then I'll walk you through exactly what we'd build and how it actually brings you clients. If it makes sense, we move forward. If not, no pressure. Fair enough?"
💡 Pro Tip: The agenda builds trust and lowers their guard. "No pressure" takes the edge off — which paradoxically makes them more likely to buy.

3 Dig In — Find Their Pain

▼

Ask 2–3 questions. Match to their industry:

  • "Walk me through your business real quick — how are you getting clients right now and where do you feel like you're leaving money on the table?"
  • "Do you have a website right now? Is it actually bringing you business or is it just kind of… there?"
  • "If someone Googles what you do in your area, are they finding you — or your competitor?"
  • "What made you book this call? What's the #1 thing you want to fix?"
  • "How are new clients finding you right now — is it all referrals, or do you have anything working for you online?"
  • "When a prospect is shopping around and they look you up next to another agent — what do they see? Does your site close that deal for you?"
  • "Are you set up to capture leads after hours, or does everything stop when your office closes?"
  • "When a high-value prospect is vetting you before that first meeting, what are they finding online? Does it match the level of service you actually provide?"
  • "Do you have anything in place right now that's booking consultations for you — or is it all manual outreach?"
  • "Is your site SEC and FINRA compliant, or is that something you've been meaning to figure out?"
  • "When a seller is deciding between you and two other agents, what does your website say about you compared to theirs?"
  • "Are you getting any inbound leads from your site, or is it basically a digital business card right now?"
  • "What happens when someone visits your site at 10pm — can they book with you, or do they just bounce?"
💡 Pro Tip: "What made you book this call?" is the most powerful question. Their answer IS the pain — use their exact words later when you present the solution.

4 Show the Gap

▼
"So here's what I'm hearing — you're great at what you do, but your website isn't doing anything for you. It's not bringing in leads, it's not booking calls, it's not working while you sleep. Right now you're the one doing all the heavy lifting to get clients — and your site should be handling a big chunk of that for you."

Industry-specific gap amplifiers:

"The agents crushing it in your market have sites that capture leads 24/7 — quote requests coming in, chatbots qualifying prospects at midnight, follow-ups going out automatically. Meanwhile, most agents have a site that just sits there. That's the gap — and that's exactly what we fix."
"Your competitors who are growing the fastest? Their website is doing the selling before the prospect even gets on the phone. Compliance built in, consultations booking automatically, content that positions them as the authority. If your site isn't doing that, you're losing high-value clients to someone whose site is."
"Every seller is looking up agents online before they make that call. The one with the site that looks professional, has real credibility, and makes it easy to take the next step — that's who gets the listing. If your site isn't set up to do that, the business is going to someone else."
💡 Pro Tip: Use their exact words from discovery. "You said [X] — that's the gap." When they hear their own words back, it hits different.

5 Present the Solution

▼
"Here's what we'd build for you — a fully custom website designed to actually bring you clients. Not a template, not a DIY site — a conversion-first system with built-in lead capture, SEO so people find you on Google, AI chatbot that qualifies leads and books calls for you 24/7, and automation so no lead ever slips through the cracks. Everything is built around your business, your market, and your compliance requirements. Let me show you what that looks like..."
💡 Pro Tip: This is where you share your screen, show examples, walk through their specific deliverables. Tailor it to their pain from Step 3. Use the Quick Links tab to pull up portfolio sites and live examples.

6 Close the Deal

▼
"So [Name], you told me [their pain point] — and I just showed you exactly how we solve that. The investment starts at just [price], we build everything, we handle everything, and in 4 to 6 weeks you've got a system that's actually working for your business. What questions do you have before we lock in your launch date?"

If they're feeling it — push for the full close today:

"Here's what I'd love to do right now — I'm going to send over a project overview so you can see exactly what's included, the pricing, the timeline, everything laid out clean. Take a quick look, and if it all checks out, we can knock out the final proposal right here on this call and lock in your build slot. The final proposal is the last step — once that's signed, we're off to the races. Sound good?"
💡 Pro Tip: "What questions do you have before we lock in your launch date?" assumes the close. Don't ask "So what do you think?" — that invites hesitation. The overview is the soft commitment, the final proposal is the real close. Push to complete both on this call.

After they review the overview:

"Everything look good? Perfect. Let's get the final proposal done right now — it takes two minutes. This locks in your launch date and gets the team started. And just so you know, it's only 50% to get started — that secures your build slot and kicks everything off. The rest doesn't come until you've approved the direction and we deliver."
💡 Note on payment: Projects under $1,500 require full payment at signing. Everything above that is 50% to start, 30% at design approval, 20% at final delivery. Always frame the 50% as securing their spot — not a cost, an investment to lock in their timeline.

7 Lock In & Next Steps

▼

If they complete the final proposal on the call:

"You're all set, [Name]. Here's what happens now — once payment goes through, you'll automatically get an invite to your own Client Portal. That's where you can message us directly through chat, upload any files we need — logos, headshots, content — and check your project progress in real time. Our team gets your strategy kickoff scheduled from there, we map out your entire build, and you'll start seeing things come together fast. Welcome aboard — you're gonna love this."

If they want to review the overview first:

"Totally understand — here's what I'll do. I'm sending the overview to your inbox right now so you can look it over, show your partner, whatever you need. And I'll have the final proposal over to you before end of day so everything's ready to go the moment you are. Once you sign off on the proposal, we lock in your launch date and get started immediately."
💡 Pro Tip: The overview builds trust, the final proposal closes the deal. Your goal is to get both done on this call. If they push back, don't schedule another call — just send both by end of day. That keeps momentum alive without adding friction. When the proposal lands in their inbox ready to sign, most close within 24 hours.

Sales Playbook — 10 Principles

Principle 01

Tonality Over Script

How you say it matters 10x more than what you say. A confident, conversational tone creates trust. Reading a script creates distance. Master the message, then forget the words.

Principle 02

Ask, Don't Tell

Questions control conversations. The person asking questions is always in control. Discovery isn't a checklist — it's a conversation that makes the prospect sell themselves on needing help.

Principle 03

Silence Is a Weapon

After you make a key point or ask a closing question — shut up. The first person who talks loses. Silence creates pressure that makes the prospect process and respond honestly.

Principle 04

Sell the Outcome, Not the Product

Nobody buys branding. They buy more clients, more trust, more authority. Paint the picture of their life AFTER working with you. Features bore people — results close deals.

Principle 05

Use Their Own Words

When the prospect tells you their pain in discovery, write it down. Then use their exact words when you present the solution. "You said you're tired of X — that's exactly what we fix." It hits 10x harder than your version.

Principle 06

Pain Over Features

Nobody buys branding. They buy the solution to their pain: lost deals, weak online presence, blending in with competitors. Find the pain, amplify it, then present the bridge.

Principle 07

Assume the Close

"What questions do you have before we get started?" is 10x stronger than "So what do you think?" Assume they're moving forward. Handle objections as they come — don't invite them.

Principle 08

Objections = Interest

A prospect who objects is a prospect who's engaged. Someone truly not interested just ends the call. Every objection is an invitation to go deeper. Welcome them.

Principle 09

Speed to Follow-Up

Send the overview within 60 seconds of ending the call. Text a confirmation within 5 minutes. Speed signals professionalism and keeps the momentum alive. Every minute you wait, the close rate drops.

Principle 10

Detach From the Outcome

Desperation kills deals. If you need every call to convert, you'll push too hard. Approach every call like you don't need it. Paradoxically, that confidence is exactly what makes people say yes.

Compliance & Regulatory Support

How we handle compliance across industries. Use these talking points when prospects ask about regulatory concerns.

All Industries

Our Compliance-First Approach

Every website and digital asset we build is designed with compliance in mind from day one. We work within your regulatory framework — not around it.

  • All content is reviewed for industry-specific compliance requirements before launch
  • We coordinate directly with your compliance officer or team when needed
  • Proper disclosures, disclaimers, and legal language built into every site
  • Archiving-friendly design that supports regulatory record-keeping
  • ADA accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1) included in every build
Insurance

Insurance Industry Compliance

We understand state DOI requirements, carrier guidelines, and E&O considerations that insurance professionals face with marketing materials.

  • State-specific licensing disclosures and required legal language
  • Carrier brand guideline compliance — we work within co-branding rules
  • E&O-conscious language — no guarantees, no misleading claims
  • NAIC advertising regulation adherence for digital marketing
  • Privacy policy and data collection compliance (state-specific where needed)
  • Proper disclaimer placement for quotes, coverage descriptions, and testimonials
Financial Advisors

Financial Services Compliance

We build for SEC, FINRA, and state-level regulatory requirements. Whether you're an RIA, IAR, or BD-affiliated advisor, we've got you covered.

  • SEC/FINRA advertising rule compliance (Rule 206(4)-1, Rule 2210)
  • Required disclosures: ADV Part 2A brochure links, CRS references
  • Testimonial and endorsement compliance under the SEC Marketing Rule
  • Performance presentation guidelines — no cherry-picked returns
  • BD/RIA pre-approval workflow support — we submit for review before publish
  • Social media archiving compatibility for compliance record-keeping
  • Anti-fraud provisions and fair disclosure requirements
Real Estate / Mortgage

Real Estate & Mortgage Compliance

From RESPA and TILA to state licensing display requirements — we make sure your digital presence meets every regulation.

  • RESPA compliance for mortgage marketing — no kickback-triggering language
  • TILA/Regulation Z advertising requirements for rate/APR disclosures
  • NMLS number display requirements on all mortgage marketing
  • State real estate commission licensing disclosure requirements
  • Fair Housing Act compliant language and imagery
  • Equal Housing Opportunity logos and required disclaimers
  • TCPA compliance for lead capture and automated follow-up
All Industries

What We Don't Cover (Third-Party Responsibilities)

We build and optimize — but certain operational costs and third-party platforms remain the client's responsibility:

  • Web hosting charges (Shopify, WordPress hosting, etc.)
  • Domain registration and renewal fees
  • Third-party platform subscriptions (Twilio, chatbot providers, CRM licenses)
  • Email marketing platform fees (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, etc.)
  • SSL certificates (if not included with hosting)
  • Paid advertising spend (Google Ads, Meta Ads, etc.)
  • Professional photography or stock image licensing

We integrate and configure all of these systems for you — we just don't cover the subscription costs themselves. Our Monthly Management Plans handle the ongoing optimization and maintenance.
