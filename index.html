1
Open — Build Rapport Opener
▼
Say This
"Hey what's going on, appreciate you reaching out. Tell me a little about what you've got going right now."
Pro tip: Shut up after this. Let them talk. The more they share, the more ammo you have. Don't pitch yet — just listen and take notes on their pain points.
2
Find the Problem Discovery
▼
Ask These (Pick 2–3)
  • "Are you getting consistent leads from your site right now?"
  • "Do you feel like your site actually converts visitors or just exists?"
  • "How are people finding you right now — referrals, organic, paid?"
  • "If I pulled up your site right now, would you say it represents the level of business you're trying to build?"
What you're listening for: "No," "not really," "I'm not sure." Any hesitation means there's a gap. Mirror their language back — if they say "my site is trash," use that exact word later.
3
Identify the Gap Bridge
▼
Say This
"So it sounds like you've got [traffic/a site/some presence], but it's not really turning into consistent business. That's exactly the gap I help agencies close."
Key move: Repeat their problem back in their own words. This builds trust instantly because they feel understood. Don't exaggerate — just reflect what they told you. This is where the sale actually starts.
4
Position the Solution Pitch
▼
Say This
"What I do is build out your entire digital presence — site, SEO, lead capture, the works — so your site actually brings in and converts leads on autopilot. Everything stays compliant, everything's built to perform."
Don't oversell. Keep it tight. You're not listing features — you're painting the outcome. They don't care about "responsive design." They care about getting more clients without chasing referrals.
5
Transition to Close Bridge
▼
Say This
"Based on what you told me, this is something I can definitely help you fix. Let me walk you through what it looks like."
Confidence is everything here. Don't say "I think I can help." Say "I can fix this." You've heard their problem, you know the solution — own it. This is the bridge from discovery to dollars.
6
Drop the Price Pitch
▼
Say This
"Based on what you need, this falls around $X. We do 40% upfront to get started, and the rest is spread out. Nothing crazy — just enough to get the ball rolling."
State the price and stop talking. Don't justify it, don't discount it, don't fill the silence. The first person who talks after the price loses. Let them process. Silence is your best closer.
7
Close Close
▼
Say This
"Let's go ahead and get you set up so we can start fixing this."
Assumptive close. You're not asking "do you want to move forward?" — you're assuming they do. Then pause. Let them respond. If they push back, that's what the objections tab is for.
If No Sale — Leave-Open Close
"No worries at all — I never push decisions. At least now you know exactly what's holding your site back. When you're ready to fix it, I'll already have the game plan ready to go."
"I'll send you a quick recap so you have everything in front of you. If something clicks later this week, just reply and we'll pick it right back up — no need to re-explain anything."
Plant the seed, don't beg. This keeps the door open without sounding desperate. Follow up in 48 hours with a short text or email — not a pitch, just a check-in.

Objection Handling

Every objection is a buying signal. They're still on the phone — that means they're interested. Handle it, don't fight it.

!
"I need to think about it"
▼
"Totally understand — what specifically are you unsure about? Is it the investment, the timing, or something else?"
Why this works: Forces them to name the real objection instead of hiding behind a vague excuse. Most of the time "I need to think about it" means "I have a concern I haven't said out loud." Get it out in the open and you can actually address it.
$
"It's too expensive"
▼
"I hear you — compared to what you're getting right now, does what I'm offering actually solve the problem or not?"
Why this works: Reframes price as value. You're not defending the cost — you're making them compare your solution to their current results (which are zero). The answer is almost always "yes, it solves it." Now price becomes an investment, not an expense.
↻
"I already have someone"
▼
"Got you — are they actually driving results, or just maintaining what's already there?"
Why this works: If their current person was crushing it, they wouldn't be on a strategy call with you. This question makes them confront the gap between what they're paying for and what they're actually getting. Let them sell themselves on switching.
?
"Do you have case studies?"
▼
"Great question — I'm in a relaunch phase onboarding a few agencies right now to build fresh case studies. That actually works in your favor because you're getting premium, hands-on work at a lower entry point than what this will cost in 6 months."
Why this works: Turns a weakness into urgency. You're not apologizing — you're positioning them as getting early access to something that's about to be more expensive. Scarcity + exclusivity = action.
⏳
"Not right now"
▼
"No worries — timing has to make sense. Just keep in mind, every month your site isn't converting is money you're leaving on the table. When you're ready, I'll have the plan ready to go."
Why this works: Respects their decision while planting a cost-of-inaction seed. You're not arguing — you're letting the math do the work. Follow up in 5–7 days with a low-pressure check-in.

Sales Playbook

🎯 The 80/20 Rule

They should be talking 80% of the time. Your job is to ask the right questions and shut up. The more they talk, the more they convince themselves they need you.

🪞 Mirror Their Words

If they say "my site looks outdated," don't say "your site needs modernization." Say "outdated" right back. Matching their language builds subconscious trust.

⏸️ Master the Pause

After you state the price — stop. After you ask a closing question — stop. Silence is uncomfortable. The first person to break it loses leverage. Let them fill the gap.

🔥 Urgency Without Pressure

Never say "this deal expires." Instead: "Every month your site isn't converting, that's X leads you're not getting." Let the cost of inaction create the urgency.

📱 Follow-Up Framework

48 hours after a no-close: short text or email — "Hey [name], just wanted to follow up. No pressure, just want to make sure you have everything you need." Keep it human.

🚫 Never Trash Competitors

If they have someone already, don't talk bad about their current provider. Just ask if they're getting results. Let the gap speak for itself — you don't need to push them off a cliff.
