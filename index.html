1 Start Here — Open the Call

▼
"Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on — I'm excited to talk with you. Before we dive in, I want to make sure this call is all about you. I'm going to ask a few questions about your business so I can show you exactly how we'd help. Sound good?"
💡 Pro Tip: Set the frame early — you're leading this call. "Sound good?" gets a micro-yes and gives you control of the conversation from the start.

2 Set the Agenda

▼
"Here's how this will work — I'll ask you a few questions about where your business is now, then I'll show you exactly what we'd build for you and how it drives real results. If it makes sense, we'll talk about next steps. If not, no hard feelings. Fair?"
💡 Pro Tip: The agenda builds trust and lowers their guard. "No hard feelings" takes the pressure off — which paradoxically makes them more likely to buy.

3 Dig In — Find Their Pain

▼

Ask 2–3 questions. Match to their industry:

  • "Tell me about your business — what's working well right now and what's not?"
  • "When someone looks you up online right now, are you proud of what they see?"
  • "Where are most of your clients coming from today — and is that consistent?"
  • "What made you book this call? What's the #1 thing you're trying to fix?"
  • "Are your referral partners sending you consistent business, or is it hit or miss?"
  • "How are you standing out from every other agent in your market right now?"
  • "When a prospect compares you to a competitor online, what do they see?"
  • "How are you building trust with prospects before the first meeting?"
  • "Do your high-net-worth prospects research you online before committing?"
  • "What does your digital first impression say about your advisory practice?"
  • "In a market with so many agents, how are you differentiating yourself?"
  • "When a seller checks you out before the listing appointment, what do they find?"
  • "Are you getting leads from your online presence, or mostly referrals and cold outreach?"
💡 Pro Tip: "What made you book this call?" is the most powerful question. Their answer IS the pain — use their exact words later when you present the solution.

4 Show the Gap

▼
"So based on what you're telling me — you're doing [X], but you're not getting the [leads / trust / visibility / growth] you deserve. That gap between where you are now and where you know you should be — that's exactly what we close."

Industry-specific gap amplifiers:

"Right now, your competitors who invest in branding are the ones getting the referrals, the Google searches, and the trust — before the prospect even picks up the phone. That's the gap we close for you."
"High-net-worth clients aren't Googling 'financial advisor near me' and picking the first one. They're vetting you. If your online presence doesn't match your expertise, you're losing six-figure clients before you even know they were looking."
"Every seller has 10 agents in their inbox. The one who looks the most professional online — polished brand, strong website, real authority — that's who gets the listing appointment. If that's not you right now, it's your competitor."
💡 Pro Tip: Use their exact words from discovery. "You said you're tired of [X] — that's the gap." When they hear their own words back, it hits different.

5 Present the Solution

▼
"Here's what we'd do for you — we build a complete premium brand system: your identity, your website, your content, your entire online presence — designed to make you the obvious choice in your market. This isn't a template. This is custom-built around your business, your market, and your goals. Let me walk you through what that looks like..."
💡 Pro Tip: This is where you share your screen, show examples, walk through their specific deliverables. Tailor it to their pain from Step 3. Use the Quick Links tab to pull up relevant pages live.

6 Close the Deal

▼
"So [Name], based on everything we talked about — you said you want [their pain point], and I just showed you exactly how we solve that. The investment is [price], and we handle everything from start to finish. What questions do you have before we get started?"

If they're ready:

"Let's do it. I'll send over the proposal right now — you'll have everything you need in your inbox in the next 60 seconds. Once you sign off, we kick things off immediately."
💡 Pro Tip: "What questions do you have before we get started?" assumes the close. Don't ask "So what do you think?" — that invites hesitation. Assume the yes and handle objections as they come.

7 Lock In & Next Steps

▼
"Perfect — here's what happens next: I'm sending the proposal to your email right now. Once you review and sign, we'll schedule your brand kickoff call within 48 hours. From there, we handle everything. You're going to love what we build for you, [Name]."

If they need time:

"Totally understand — I'll send the proposal over so you can look at it on your own time. When would be a good time to reconnect? I want to make sure I'm available for any questions. How about [day]?"
💡 Pro Tip: Always leave with a specific follow-up date. "I'll follow up next week" is a dead lead. "Let's reconnect Thursday at 2" keeps the deal alive. Use the Send Proposal tab to fire it instantly.

"I need to think about it." Universal

▼
"Totally respect that — I'd never want you to rush. Just so I understand, when you say 'think about it,' is it the investment, the timing, or whether this is the right fit? That way I can get you exactly what you need to make a decision."

"It's too expensive / I don't have the budget." Universal

▼
"I hear you — and honestly, that's the #1 thing our clients worried about before they started. But here's what they found: not investing in their brand was costing them more in lost deals than the investment itself. If this brings you even 2-3 new clients, what's that worth to your business?"

"I already have a website." Universal

▼
"That's great — having a website is step one. But the question is: is it actually working for you? Is it bringing in leads, building trust, and making people choose you over competitors? There's a big difference between a website and a brand that converts."

"I need to talk to my partner / spouse." Universal

▼
"Absolutely — that's smart. Just so you can have the best conversation with them, let me send the proposal over with everything we discussed. That way you can walk them through it. Would it help if I hopped on a quick call with both of you? I'm happy to answer their questions directly."
💡 Pro Tip: Offering to get on a call with the decision maker shows confidence and keeps you in the loop instead of letting the prospect sell it for you (they won't).

"Can you send me the proposal and I'll look it over?" Universal

▼
"Of course — I'll fire it over right now. Before I do, is there anything specific you want me to include or any questions I can answer right now while we're both here? I want to make sure it addresses everything we talked about."
💡 Pro Tip: Don't just send and pray. Get their real objection before they hang up. Then set a specific follow-up: "Let's touch base Thursday — 10am or 2pm work?"

"I've been burned before by agencies." Universal

▼
"I'm sorry to hear that — and honestly, that's one of the biggest reasons people come to us. Most agencies are generic, slow, and don't understand your industry. We specialize exclusively in [insurance/financial/real estate] professionals. That's all we do. And you've already seen the quality of what we build — this isn't a gamble."

"I get all my business from referrals." Universal

▼
"That's awesome — referrals mean you're great at what you do. But here's the thing: even your referrals are Googling you before they call. If your online presence doesn't match the reputation your referral partner gave you, you're losing deals you don't even know about. We make sure when people look you up, they're blown away."

"How are you different from other agencies?" Universal

▼
"Great question. Three things: 1) We only work with [industry] professionals — we're not a generalist agency trying to figure out your market. 2) We build everything — brand, website, content, marketing — as one system, not piecemeal. 3) Our clients actually see results because we focus on conversion, not just making things look pretty."

"I can just do it myself / use DIY tools." Universal

▼
"You definitely could — Canva, Wix, all that stuff exists. But let me ask: would you tell your clients to handle their own [insurance/finances/real estate] because YouTube tutorials exist? Probably not. Your expertise is your business — ours is making your brand the most trusted name in your market."

"The timing isn't right." Universal

▼
"I totally get that. But here's the thing — your competitors aren't waiting. Every day without a strong brand is another day prospects are choosing them over you. The best time to build your brand was a year ago. The second best time is right now. And we make the process completely hands-off for you."

"My carrier provides marketing." Insurance

▼
"They do — and so does every other agent's carrier. That's the problem. When every agent in your market has the same carrier templates, who stands out? Nobody. A custom brand is what separates the top 1% of agents from the rest."

"My book of business is big enough." Insurance

▼
"That's great — but are you retaining those clients as well as you'd like? A strong brand doesn't just attract new clients, it keeps existing ones loyal. When your clients see competitors marketing to them, your brand is the reason they stay."

"I only work with referrals from agents." Insurance

▼
"That's a great channel — but even the agents referring you are looking you up online before they send their clients your way. If your brand doesn't look the part, you're missing referrals you'll never know about."

"Compliance won't approve it." Financial

▼
"Totally understand — compliance is real. We've worked with advisors across multiple BDs and RIAs. We build everything to be compliance-friendly and work directly with your compliance team if needed. You won't have to worry about that part."

"My BD provides a website." Financial

▼
"They do — and it looks exactly like every other advisor's at your BD. When a prospect with $2M to invest is comparing three advisors and all three have the same cookie-cutter BD site, who do they pick? The one with a real brand."

"My AUM is big enough, I don't need marketing." Financial

▼
"That's impressive. But here's the question: if you could attract higher-net-worth clients or build a recognizable brand that commands premium fees, wouldn't that be worth it? We're not about volume — we're about positioning you for the next level."

"I just use Zillow / Realtor.com for leads." Real Estate

▼
"Those platforms work — but you're renting leads where you're competing with 20 other agents for the same prospect. A strong brand means people come to YOU directly. Zillow leads cost money and loyalty. Your brand builds equity."

"My brokerage handles my marketing." Real Estate

▼
"Your brokerage markets the brokerage — not you. When you leave or switch, that marketing goes away. Your personal brand follows you everywhere. The top producers in every market have their own brand identity."

"The market is slow right now." Real Estate

▼
"That's exactly when you should be investing in your brand. When the market is slow, the agents who show up looking professional and trustworthy get the few deals that are happening. The agents who build their brand in a downturn dominate the upswing."

Sales Playbook — 10 Principles

Principle 01

Tonality Over Script

How you say it matters 10x more than what you say. A confident, conversational tone creates trust. Reading a script creates distance. Master the message, then forget the words.

Principle 02

Ask, Don't Tell

Questions control conversations. The person asking questions is always in control. Discovery isn't a checklist — it's a conversation that makes the prospect sell themselves on needing help.

Principle 03

Silence Is a Weapon

After you make a key point or ask a closing question — shut up. The first person who talks loses. Silence creates pressure that makes the prospect process and respond honestly.

Principle 04

Sell the Outcome, Not the Product

Nobody buys branding. They buy more clients, more trust, more authority. Paint the picture of their life AFTER working with you. Features bore people — results close deals.

Principle 05

Use Their Own Words

When the prospect tells you their pain in discovery, write it down. Then use their exact words when you present the solution. "You said you're tired of X — that's exactly what we fix." It hits 10x harder than your version.

Principle 06

Pain Over Features

Nobody buys branding. They buy the solution to their pain: lost deals, weak online presence, blending in with competitors. Find the pain, amplify it, then present the bridge.

Principle 07

Assume the Close

"What questions do you have before we get started?" is 10x stronger than "So what do you think?" Assume they're moving forward. Handle objections as they come — don't invite them.

Principle 08

Objections = Interest

A prospect who objects is a prospect who's engaged. Someone truly not interested just ends the call. Every objection is an invitation to go deeper. Welcome them.

Principle 09

Speed to Follow-Up

Send the proposal within 60 seconds of ending the call. Text a confirmation within 5 minutes. Speed signals professionalism and keeps the momentum alive. Every minute you wait, the close rate drops.

Principle 10

Detach From the Outcome

Desperation kills deals. If you need every call to convert, you'll push too hard. Approach every call like you don't need it. Paradoxically, that confidence is exactly what makes people say yes.
